Recent Result

I am leading the Hyper Suprime-Cam Survey effort to search for low-surface-brightness galaxies (LSBGs). We recently discovered \({\sim}\)800 LSBGs in the first 200 deg\(^2\) of the survey. Check our paper on the arXiv. The software used to carry out this search is open-source and on github.

LSBGs are a beautiful, diverse population of galaxies, ranging from the faintest dwarf spheroidals to giant spirals like Malin 1. Below I show some cutouts of blue and red LSBGs from our sample.

To give you a sense for how deep the Hyper Suprime-Cam (HSC) images are, below I compare images from HSC and SDSS of some of the larger (in angular size) galaxies in our sample. Interestingly, these objects are also detected the ultraviolet, suggesting ongoing star formation in these systems. This can be seen in the GALEX images in the bottom row.
